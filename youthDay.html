16 June 1976

Thousands of black youth took to the streets of the (then)Transvaal now known as Gauteng to protest against the regime who had introduced Afrikaans as the medium of instruction along side English, in black township schools.

How Many attended

It is stimated that around 20 000 students attended the rally

Arrival of the Police

The police was called in to disperse the crowd and as a result the peaceful protest took a turn for the worst. Police arrived on scene with dogs and started using tear gas. When the crowd fought back they started using bullets to control the crowd.

Mayhem

Over 500 young people died on this fateful day and would go down in history as one of the most brutal apartheid memories in South Afrikan History.

Gone but never forgotten

Among the first children killed was 15-year-old Hastings Ndlovu, and 12-year-old Hector Pieterson famously depicted in a black and white image of young man holding another who had been injured, carrying him to safety. This picture depicted below is one of the images synonymous with June 16th and reminds us of the innocent lives lost this day.

What youth day means to South Africans

Although many innocent lives were lost on June 16th, it also symbolises the struggle and the battle we overcame from the oppression. June 16th is a day we will never forget but we can also celebrate to acknowledge our freedom as a democratic country.
